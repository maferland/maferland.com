## The bet

Recipe storage is a solved, one-time problem — you fill a recipe box once and stop opening the app. That is why the category churns and why most recipe apps are pay-once utilities. The bet is that **meal planning is a recurring weekly job**, and a product built around that job gets opened every Sunday.

Hearth started from tearing down a competitor and noticing that AI recipe parsing — the headline feature everyone ships — is already a commodity. The only durable advantage is a **non-portable intelligence layer**: the household's taste graph, its plan history, the "what's for dinner" that learns what these two people actually eat. Recipes stay portable; that part does not.

## Why I am building it

It cleared the gate on one dimension above all: I would use it weekly, with my partner, starting now. I already have an AI assistant that generates the recipes. The whole thesis is dogfood-first — if my own household does not open it unprompted for three weeks, the idea is wrong and it goes to the graveyard. Cheaply.

## The honest risk

There is no moat in what ships first. If the weekly habit and the taste graph never materialize, Hearth is a commodity recipe box that churns like all the others. The build has to reach the habit-forming stage before low-frequency churn sets in. That is the bet.

Building in private during the dogfood phase. If it survives the household, it graduates here to a link.
